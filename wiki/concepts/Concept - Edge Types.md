# Concept: Edge Types

In AI-native knowledge graphs, "edge types" define the nature of relationships between notes or nodes. Unlike simple links, edge types clarify how two pieces of information are connected, enabling more precise AI reasoning. Common edge types include:

- **Supports**: This argument or fact supports another.
- **Contradicts**: This information disagrees with another.
- **Depends On**: One node requires another to be true.
- **Derived From**: Created based on another idea or fact.
- **Part Of**: Indicates membership in a larger structure or process.
- **Authored**: Identifies the creator (human or AI).
- **Preceded By / Followed By**: Sequence relationships.
- **Related To**: General association.
- **Tagging**: Flexible, catch-all relationship.

Explicit edge types make knowledge graphs more useful for both AI and humans, supporting advanced queries and synthesis.
