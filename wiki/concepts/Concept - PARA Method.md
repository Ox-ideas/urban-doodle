# Concept: PARA Method

The PARA Method (Projects, Areas, Resources, Archives) is a popular personal knowledge management (PKM) framework developed by [[Tiago Forte]]. It organizes notes and files into four broad categories for human usability. In the context of AI-native knowledge management, PARA is critiqued as being too coarse and human-centric, lacking the granularity and explicit structure needed for optimal AI reasoning.

- **Projects**: Active tasks and deliverables.
- **Areas**: Ongoing responsibilities.
- **Resources**: Reference material.
- **Archives**: Inactive or completed items.

The "Infinite Brain" system proposes a more granular, typed, and AI-friendly alternative.
